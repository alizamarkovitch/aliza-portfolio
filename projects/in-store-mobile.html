In-Store Mobile Experience

Digital wallet for mobile coffee app

Overview

Tim Hortons is the largest quick-service restaurant chain in Canada with 4.9K restaurants in 15 countries worldwide. As a product designer on the Ecosystem team, I led the redesign of the mobile experiences, focusing on reimagining the future state of the mobile app.

The outcome was a fresh interface with new services and features. This case study outlines the UX redesign of 'Scan': a feature for mobile payments and rewards.

Goal

Reimagine the in-store mobile experience

The Challenge

What is Scan?

Scan is a mobile payments feature for the in-store experience. It is the most used feature in the mobile application. Our mission was to reduce purchase friction by consolidating payment-related activities in one place. The key tasks included:

  • Scan to pay
  • Manage rewards
  • Edit payment methods

Pain Points

  • Earning rewards and mobile payments are separate actions and require users to scan twice during in-store checkout.
  • Payment and reward settings are spread across applications. Need to consolidate.
  • Many different UI components for editing settings. Slows down cognitive load (ie. tabs, toggles, and text links).

Research findings and insights

The Solution

One Tap to Scan, Pay and Earn

Previously the QR lived on the consumer app. However, research showed that the most common design pattern is a customer scanning a vendor's QR code.

This updated interaction speeds up the in-store check-out experience and reduces scanning issues related to vendor devices. Scan pay and earn points in one tap.

Final design screens showing the new payment flow

All payment settings in one place

Data analytics highlighted a correlation between scanning time and changing reward tiers. Moreover, 160K customers edit rewards levels per day.

Previously payment-related features were siloed into different areas of the app. The Wallet consolidates all payment-related activities. Customers can easily manage payments, reward settings, and transaction activity all in one place.

Usability testing session

A cleaner interface for a smoother mobile payment experience

Scan the fast action button (FAB) for quick access to Express Pay. Updated UI components provide a cleaner interface for editing reward and payment settings.

Early wireframes exploring payment flow

Research

I began by understanding the purpose of every element within the current design. Which information is the most important? Which could be removed. I cross-referenced the interface across mobile and web to highlight discrepancies. Together with stakeholders, we prioritized which content to carry over into the new design.

Research findings and insights

Wireframes

I explored different ways to simplify the payment flow. The goal was to reduce the number of steps required to complete a transaction.

Early wireframes exploring payment flow

Testing

I conducted usability testing with real users to validate the new design. The feedback helped identify areas for improvement and confirmed that the new flow was more intuitive.

Usability testing session

Final Design

The final design features a streamlined payment process, consolidated settings, and a cleaner interface for mobile payments.

Final design screens showing the new payment flow

Impact

  • Reduced in-store checkout time by consolidating payment and rewards into a single scan
  • Improved user experience by centralizing all payment-related features in one location
  • Streamlined UI components for better cognitive load and faster interactions
  • Successfully implemented across all Tim Hortons locations
